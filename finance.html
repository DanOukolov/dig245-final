Why are the Rats Racing?

How does Money Control Us?


Our financial system is dominated by Big Banks and the Federal Reserve.

If money makes the world go round, banks and the Federal Reserve make the world go round.


Definition - The Federal Reserve

The Federal Reserve is the central bank of the United States. The Fed acts independently of the US government and can affect how much money is in the economy as well as what rates banks get charged for borrowing money from the Fed.
Created in 1913

Definition - The 16th Amendment

The Congress shall have power to lay and collect taxes on incomes, from whatever source derived, without apportionment among the several States, and without regard to any census or enumeration.
Passed in 1909

Definition - Inflation

Inflation is the increase in prices of goods and services due to an increase in money supply. This happens when the Fed prints money, buys bonds, pays US bonds back, or loans more money to banks at a lower fed funds rate.
