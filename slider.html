How does Money Control Us?


Our financial system is dominated by Big Banks and the Federal Reserve.

If money makes the world go round, banks and the Federal Reserve make the world go round.


Definition - The Federal Reserve

The Federal Reserve is the central bank of the United States. The Fed acts independently of the US government and can affect how much money is in the economy as well as what rates banks get charged for borrowing money from the Fed.
Created in 1913

Definition - The 16th Amendment

The Congress shall have power to lay and collect taxes on incomes, from whatever source derived, without apportionment among the several States, and without regard to any census or enumeration.
Passed in 1909

Definition - Inflation

Inflation is the increase in prices of goods and services due to an increase in money supply. This happens when the Fed prints money, buys bonds, pays US bonds back, or loans more money to banks at a lower fed funds rate.
Economic Principle

Definition - Fiscal vs. Moneytary Policy

Fiscal policy is a legislative movement of our government to stimulate our economy. They can change the tax codes, use fiscal stimuli, or offer subsidies. Monetary policies are the actions of the Fed.

Thomas Piketty



Thomas Piketty is a French economist who is borderline Marxist. However, his theories are a great explanation of how the wealth gap has grown in the United States since World War 2. He theorizes that the United States is currently primed for a wealth redistribution since we have reached late stage capitalism and wealth has accumulated to proportions that are comparable to the 1920's. He forecasts a major depression if we do not redistribute.

Loopholes Galore!



In addition to falling tax bracket percentages and required income to qualify for the upper bracket, the wealthy are granted a million different holes to hide money in. From writing off agregiously bizarre "business expenses" to long term public securities holdings, the wealthy are able to get away with lower rates than that of middle class families. The politicians who they donate to write and approve these codes year over year.

This does not even include those wealthy individuals that have even shadier ways to shuffle money out of taxes with shell companies, offshore accounts, trust funds, and once again, "business expenses and holdings."
